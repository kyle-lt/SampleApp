Try this:

1. You’ve just sold 5 apples! Click “Sell One” 5 times.

2. It’s the end of the day and your suppliers are refreshing your inventory. Click “Restock” to reset your initial stock back to 100 apples, 100 oranges, 100 bananas, and 100 lemons.

3. Bananas have just come in season and you found a supplier. Click “Stock Bananas" to add them to your inventory list.

4. Your orange supplier is going out of business, so you won’t be selling them at the fruit stand. Click “Remove Oranges" to remove them from the inventory list.


Each of these operations is automatically tracked by AppDynamics as a Business Transaction.

What is a Business Transaction

A Business Transaction represents the end-to-end processing path used to fulfill a service request in the monitored environment. The Agents monitoring the sample app have sent data to the AppDynamics Controller.

In the Business Transaction List view, you can see all the Business Transactions discovered by AppDynamics, and basic metrics like load (Calls/min) and the average response time it takes to execute.

Note: It takes up to 2 minutes for Business Transactions and metrics to update.



Application Flow Map

Flow maps present a dynamic, visual representation of the components and activities of your monitored application environment. Flow maps show the tiers, nodes, message queues and databases in the environment, and the business transactions that flow through them.
